St. Expedite Press is a micropress founded in New Orleans and dedicated to the publication of literature that moves quickly, thinks slowly, and endures.

We publish fiction, translations, and visual work that resists the flattening pressures of the contemporary market: literature that refuses to become content, slogans, or lifestyle branding.

Orientation

Our editorial orientation is best described as soft Catholic, postpolitical, and postidentitarian. This does not mean doctrinal or partisan alignment. It means a seriousness about meaning, memory, ritual, and moral consequence.

We believe literature should be allowed to speak in symbols, parables, and contradictions—to explore grace, failure, heresy, devotion, and doubt without being conscripted into contemporary ideological regimes.

What We Publish

St. Expedite Press publishes four primary bodies of work:

Reprints and rediscoveries from the Southern and Gulf tradition, especially works that have fallen out of circulation.
European classics and modernist texts in Spanish, French, and Russian, translated with literary and spiritual fidelity.
LICE Magazine, a biannual journal for experimental, transgressive, and spiritually serious contemporary writing.
SEXP, our flagship fiction imprint for original novels and collections that operate outside commercial genre expectations.
Why We Exist

We exist because the literary ecosystem increasingly rewards speed, conformity, and legibility over depth.

St. Expedite Press is committed to the opposite wager: that careful work, issued in small runs, can exert influence over time; that books can still carry aura; that readers are hungry for writing that treats them as adults.

Our mission is not scale, virality, or dominance. It is continuity.

In the spirit of our patron saint, we act with urgency—but not haste. We publish what must be published, when it is ready.
