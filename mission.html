St. Expedite Press is a micropress based in New Orleans. We publish work with the belief that certain books deserve to be held, kept, and returned to. Our focus is on clarity, care, and longevity.

We concentrate on texts that have either been overlooked or never given a proper edition, and on writers who benefit from real editorial attention. Our work proceeds slowly and deliberately, with the aim of producing books that endure beyond the moment.

Orientation

Our editorial approach is rooted in a simple conviction: literature matters when it has weight. We value meaning, coherence, and a sense of moral and historical continuity.

We hold that the collapse of postmodernism has not left a void; it has uncovered the older ground of the modern—the need for form, seriousness, and the willingness to face reality without disguise.

What We Do

St. Expedite Press directs its work toward four core aims:

Curating boutique, reliable editions of texts that deserve long-term preservation and steady readership.
Offering serious editorial development for new and emerging writers, giving them a foundation of real publishing credit and oversight.
Sustaining the continuity of Southern writing—its tones, histories, and the particular ways it understands the world.
Publishing fiction and criticism grounded in the modern: work that stands on form, clarity, and consequence.
Why We Exist

Much of contemporary publishing favors speed and turnover. We take the opposite position: that slow, careful editions—printed in modest numbers—can exert influence over time.

We trust that readers still want books made with attention, and that writers deserve institutions that take their work seriously.

Our aim is not growth for its own sake. Our aim is continuity.

We publish only when the work is ready.
