# COMMONSPLACES

## Complete Document of Theory, Political Form, Platform Design, and Rollout

Commonsplaces is the platform and institutional-design horizon that extends beyond St. Expedite Press as a single imprint. It is the attempt to build canon infrastructure, literary guild coordination, and durable cultural memory under conditions where neither the market nor the university can be trusted to preserve seriousness on their own.

This document consolidates the Commonsplace material scattered across whitepapers, ontology notes, political-position papers, and institutional sketches. It is not only a platform pitch. It is a statement of what Commonsplaces is for, how it should behave, what kinds of authority it permits, and how it could move from theory to pilot form.

## I. What Commonsplaces Is

Commonsplaces is a proposed literary infrastructure with four linked layers:

1. a canon-formation and claim-tracking system
2. a federation model for presses, journals, and literary guilds
3. a governance shell for shared resources and shared procedure
4. a public-facing platform for exchange, publication, reputation, and memory

It begins from a simple refusal: culture should not be left to invisible platform metrics, collapsing institutions, or soft-academic consensus. If canon still exists, it should be made explicit. If communities are making judgments, those judgments should be declared, recorded, contestable, and durable.

## II. What Commonsplaces Is Not

Commonsplaces is not a generic social platform.

It is not:

- liberal individualism with literary decoration
- crowd rule disguised as culture
- technocracy disguised as neutrality
- platform capitalism with an arts gloss
- a grant-funded nonprofit with no machine logic
- a purely speculative token project
- a universal canon pretending to settle taste for everyone

Its stance is stricter than that.

## III. Core Thesis

The deepest Commonsplace claim is that ontology should not merely describe culture. It should record how culture temporarily agrees on what counts.

That means:

- categories are claims before they are facts
- consensus is a first-class object
- provenance matters more than rhetorical certainty
- canon is a procedural achievement
- institutional memory is part of the argument, not an afterthought

This is why Commonsplaces treats ontology as a ledger of epistemic labor. A movement label, canonical edition, influence relation, or nomination is not entered as timeless truth. It is entered as a proposal, a record, a deliberation, an uptake curve, and sometimes a freeze.

## IV. Theoretical Basis

Several theory lines converge here.

### Agent Before Institution

`Primacy of the Agent` argues that intelligibility begins from situated actors rather than from inert systems pretending to explain themselves. Commonsplaces inherits that pressure by refusing to hide judgments inside opaque structures. Somebody proposed, endorsed, rejected, froze, or revised the claim.

### Ontology as Sedimented Process

`Romanticism as Ontological Gate` pushes the stronger claim: ontology, for a cultural domain, is not a neutral map. It is the history of repeated assertions and adoptions. Commonsplaces operationalizes that principle.

### Canon Under Variance

The Audubon/Havell line in the ontology notes matters because it gives a historical model for authoritative objects made under distributed conditions. Commonsplaces does not require singular authorship in order to authorize an edition, a canon entry, or a public record. It requires declared procedure.

### Memory as Infrastructure

`Commonsplace Positionalty` and the ontology work both insist that memory is not ornament. It is a binding surface. Commonsplaces therefore favors append-only or lineage-preserving structures over clean-slate erasure.

## V. Political Persona

Commonsplaces is anti-sovereign, anti-platform, institution-restorative, procedurally conservative, and culturally aristocratic without being oligarchic.

That combination produces a specific political shape.

### Declared Authority

Authority must name itself. Hidden moderation, hidden metrics, and invisible ranking are rejected. A decision should have a source, scope, and record.

### Local and Plural Jurisdiction

No single guild or house speaks for the whole. Authority is local, overlapping, and revocable. A guild may canonize something for itself without claiming universality.

### Legitimacy Before Exchange

Commonsplaces does not assume that value emerges cleanly from frictionless exchange. It assumes value requires institutions, procedure, and memory first.

### No Clean Slate

History is constraint. Memory should not be easily erased because erasure is often how contemporary systems pretend they have solved legitimacy problems they have merely hidden.

### Closure Only Where Necessary

Commonsplaces rejects finality as a general worldview, but it allows operational closure where continuity depends on it. Canonization is the clearest example: procedure closes, interpretation continues.

## VI. Structural Model

Commonsplaces is best understood as a layered system rather than a single app.

### Layer 1: Platform

The platform is the shared environment: identity, records, guild registry, token and reputation surfaces, marketplace modules, governance tooling, and canon services.

### Layer 2: Guilds

Guilds are autonomous literary communities inside the wider environment: presses, journals, translation cells, reading societies, criticism circles, writer collectives, or venue networks.

Each guild can specialize:

- regional literature
- translation
- criticism
- poetry
- fiction
- canon study
- educational programming

### Layer 3: Council

Guilds send delegates to a Commonsplace-level council for ecosystem questions:

- admission standards
- treasury use
- platform rules
- network-wide parameters
- emergency governance

The working assumption remains one-guild-one-vote unless a stronger reason appears.

### Layer 4: Canon and Record Layer

The canon layer is where claims become durable public objects:

- nominations
- debates
- decisions
- frozen canonical references
- associated badges or honors

This layer is the true differentiator. It makes judgment legible.

## VII. Governance Logic

The underlying governance principle is subsidiarity.

- local decisions remain local
- shared infrastructure decisions escalate
- every escalation should be justified
- the broader system should not absorb local life unnecessarily

Governance therefore has at least two scales.

### Guild Governance

Each guild can choose from several patterns:

- direct voting
- elected councils
- delegated voting
- curated stewardship models
- mixed arrangements

Commonsplaces does not force ideological sameness at the guild level. It forces declared procedure.

### Commonsplace Council

The council handles:

- platform updates
- guild admissions and suspensions
- treasury allocation
- cross-guild programs
- emergency interventions

The council exists to govern connective tissue, not to flatten guild difference.

## VIII. Canon Formation

Canonization is not treated as metaphysical revelation. It is treated as a public act of durable judgment.

### Core Rule

Proposal -> deliberation -> adoption or rejection.

Each stage should leave a record.

### Canon Rules Already Implied in the Materials

- additions are possible
- subtractions should be rare or impossible once a freeze is complete
- one edition or translation should be identified for canonical reference
- anthologies are generally excluded from canonization
- interpretations remain open even when the canonical object is frozen

### Why This Matters

Most systems already canonize; they just refuse to say so. Commonsplaces makes the canon process explicit, auditable, and localizable.

## IX. AI and Service Roles

The existing Commonsplace sketches already imply three bounded machine roles.

### Eligibility Service

Checks threshold conditions:

- duplicate status
- disallowed submission types
- format completeness
- reference consistency

### Tribunal Service

Handles edge cases:

- disputed editions
- ambiguous category boundaries
- borderline eligibility
- unclear provenance

### Enforcer Service

Executes outcomes once the procedure closes:

- canonical freeze
- registry update
- badge issuance
- treasury execution where relevant

The critical limit is this: these services do not govern. They administer procedure.

## X. Economy and Support Model

Commonsplaces does not need a maximalist token economy to be coherent, but the working materials already define one.

### CASK

Fungible governance token for platform-level and guild-level participation where token-weighting is appropriate.

### PENNY

Non-transferable reputation and contribution score. This is closer to civic standing than to money.

### BADGE

Soulbound or non-transferable commemorative honors for acts, roles, wins, or milestones.

### Revenue and Support Channels

The economic layer may include:

- direct book and object sales
- guild subscriptions
- low-fee donation flows
- supporter tokens or patronage credits
- secondary royalties on scarce digital objects
- small platform fees for shared treasury maintenance

The key principle is that the system should fund creators and institutions without recreating extractive platform splits.

## XI. Program Families

Commonsplaces can host more than one kind of literary work.

### 1. Independent Press Network

Shared distribution logic, discoverability, and direct-sale infrastructure across small presses that do not want to disappear alone.

### 2. Fellowship and Incubator

A supported pipeline for writers, editors, translators, designers, or critics outside conventional credential gates.

### 3. Third Spaces and Venue Network

Physical sites for reading, screening, exhibiting, hosting, and local coordination. The goal is not just digital governance but embodied literary life.

### 4. Marketplace and Labor Exchange

Guild storefronts, job boards, editorial labor offers, commissions, and collaboration requests.

### 5. Canon and Reading Programs

Structured reading circles, canon battles, edition debates, and cross-guild curation work.

## XII. Relationship to St. Expedite Press

St. Expedite Press is the founding node and proving ground. Commonsplaces is the broader institutional horizon.

The press proves:

- editorial taste
- object production
- reader contact
- legal shell discipline
- small-scale economic reality

Commonsplaces extends those lessons outward into federated infrastructure.

The wrong order would be to build the grand system first and hope books arrive later. The right order is:

1. make a real press
2. make the press durable
3. build the infrastructure that lets multiple such institutions coexist and coordinate

## XIII. Rollout Sequence

### Phase 0: Conceptual Consolidation

Current stage.

Tasks:

- unify whitepapers and theory notes
- clarify political persona
- separate public press materials from deeper ontology documents
- identify which parts are product-ready and which remain design pressure

### Phase 1: Founding Guild Beta

Short-term target.

Tasks:

- St. Expedite Press as seed guild
- guild profile and registry
- basic donation and membership surfaces
- initial governance tests
- record model for proposals and decisions

Success condition:

- real use by a small, coherent literary node

### Phase 2: Canon Module Alpha

Tasks:

- nomination flow
- deliberation record
- freeze mechanism
- edition reference model
- manual or semi-automated tribunal handling

Success condition:

- at least one live, defensible, well-documented canonization process

### Phase 3: Multi-Guild Pilot

Tasks:

- add 2-4 aligned guilds
- convene cross-guild council
- run treasury and admissions procedure
- test differing local governance styles inside shared platform rails

Success condition:

- shared infrastructure without premature homogenization

### Phase 4: Fellowship and Venue Integration

Tasks:

- add fellowship cohorts or supported projects
- connect one or more physical venues
- test cross-guild event and publication logic

Success condition:

- Commonsplaces begins to act like an ecology, not a software demo

### Phase 5: Mature Archipelago

Longer horizon.

Tasks:

- stable treasury policy
- public grants or fiscal-shell integration if needed
- recurring festivals, salons, or coordinated releases
- canon-of-canons or cross-guild recognition layer if warranted

Success condition:

- durable multi-node literary coordination with preserved local standards

- identify seed guild use cases

### 2027

- guild beta and first canon experiments
- public explanation surfaces
- limited donation and membership flows

### 2028

- multi-guild council
- stronger marketplace and labor modules

- durable treasury procedures
- cross-guild publishing programs
- public-facing annual cycle

### 2030

- either a lean literary infrastructure with proven repeatability or a deliberate narrowing to the pieces that actually work

The horizon should remain empirical. Commonsplaces must not become a fantasy pile.

## XV. Open Design Questions

Some terms and structures remain live research rather than settled public spec.

These include:

- the final relation between guilds and deeper ontology structures such as Houses, Rings, and Runs
- the exact balance between token governance and reputation governance
- how much canon should be edition-frozen versus lineage-tracked
- whether the fiscal shell should remain LLC-adjacent, nonprofit, hybrid, or guild-specific
- where to set the boundary between public memory and private guild workspaces

These are not flaws. They are the current design frontier.

## XVI. Success Criteria

Commonsplaces succeeds if it produces the following:

1. judgments become visible instead of hidden
2. literary communities coordinate without flattening themselves into one culture
3. memory persists under disagreement
4. books, venues, fellowships, and readers meet inside declared institutional form
5. the system remains legible enough that users can tell who decided what and why

It fails if it becomes:

- a generic platform
- a token game detached from books and institutions
- a hidden hierarchy claiming procedural neutrality
- a sprawling theory machine with no live users

## XVII. Closing Position

Commonsplaces is a proposal for literary life after passive authority but before pure collapse.

It assumes:

- people still make judgments
- institutions still matter
- memory still needs carriers
- canons still form whether anyone admits it or not

Its wager is that these processes can be made explicit without being trivialized, and durable without pretending to universal sovereignty.

If St. Expedite Press is the founding node, Commonsplaces is the attempt to build an archipelago around that node without destroying what made the first node worth building.
